THEME

Finland has one of the best forest data resources in the world, but there is limited number of commercial solutions utilizing that data. Create an innovative solution by combining forest resource data with other open data sources. In this challenge, basic data sets are offered by Finnish Forest Centre, National Land Survey of Finland (MML), Natural Resources Institute Finland (LUKE) and Finnish Environment Institute (SYKE). You may utilize these in your solution, but you are not limited to those. End-users can be — but are not limited to — forest industry, forest owners, other forest stakeholders or citizens.

Solution domains we appreciate

  • Forestry and Forest Resource Data: 1. The visualization and demonstration of the forest by using for instance LiDAR data, 2. Enriching the open forest data with crowdsourcing technologies, 3. Theme maps based on the end user preferences and points of interests, 4. Felling potential evaluation for the forest/public roads. Evaluating also areas, where road network needs to be established/improved based on the felling potential, 5. The simulation of the forest growth based on possible forest operations enabling also the simulation of the impacts in carbon sinks for young forests.
  • Tourism and Related Ecosystem Services: A mobile forest navigator providing the optimal route search in the forest. Enriched with the virtual reality elements.
  • Protection of Waters: GPS based electronic guide for the protection of waters, which provides a guidance for protecting the waters when planning forest operations. It helps to plan the protection of waters in advance and prior to any forestry operations for instance based on the terrain shapes.
  • Technologies suggested: Virtual Reality, Crowdsourcing, Mobile Application, Big Data and multiple data sources, IOT/Sensors.

Constraints

Every solution has to utilize the open forest data. You can access the open forest data here: https://www.metsaan.fi/paikkatietoaineisto

About the company / Background

MINISTRY OF AGRICULTURE AND FORESTRY: The Ministry of Agriculture and Forestry steers the policy on sustainable use of natural resources. Legislative work is carried out as part of the Finnish Government and the EU institutions and decision-making. Renewable natural resources derive from the renewal capacity of the nature itself. Among other things, they comprise all plants and animals. According to the principle of sustainable development, renewable natural resources must be used so that their value is preserved for the future: meeting the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs. More about The Ministry of Agriculture and Forestry: http://mmm.fi/en/ministry

FINNISH FOREST CENTRE – FOCUSING ON PEOPLE AND FOREST: Suomen metsäkeskus, The Finnish Forest Centre is a state-funded organisation covering the whole country. We are tasked with promoting forestry and related livelihoods, advising landowners on how to care for and benefit from their forests and the ecosystems therein, collecting and sharing data related to Finland's forests and enforcing forestry legislation. More about The Finnish Forest Centre: https://www.metsakeskus.fi/

DATABIO - DATA DRIVEN BIOECONOMY: The Data-Driven Bioeconomy project (DataBio) focuses on the production of best possible raw materials from agriculture, forestry and fishery for the bioeconomy industry to produce food, energy and biomaterials taking into account responsibility and sustainability. In order to meet the above objectives, DataBio is controlling and putting to use the innovative ICTs and information flows centered mostly around the use of proximal and remote sensors, in order to provide a streamlined Big Data Infrastructure for data discovery, retrieval, processing and visualizing, in support to decisions in bioeconomy business operations. More about the DATABio project: https://www.databio.eu/en/

Challenge

Create and bundle new technology and smart mobility services to make it easier for event attendees to reach events and move around the city during events and travel home happily. Selected event organizers and venues will be available for developers to test their solutions earliest in Q3-4/2018.

Solution examples

  • Before the event(-s): Solutions which utilize smart mobility services for event and city marketing purposes, such as attendee engagement and interaction
  • Before the event(-s): Solutions which that utilize smart mobility services to make actual trip to an event happen smoother
  • During the event(-s): Solutions which bundle other event and city services to movement around the city
  • During the event(-s): Solutions which enhance the event experience
  • For event management: Solutions which help event organizers manage added services to their events more easily and effectively
  • For event management: Smarter ticket systems, which help bundle services to events and drive audience interaction
  • After the event: Solutions which utilize smart mobility services for event long term audience engagement
  • After the event: Solutions which utilize smart mobility services to make trip back home smooth and highlight the positive event experience

Constraints

  • Platforms are preferred over event apps.
  • New standalone solutions without any connection to existing services are not preferred.

Challenge

Create solutions for people commuting between Tallinn and Helsinki weekly. Come up with new innovative services and solutions that make the commute smoother, shorter and more comfortable. You can extend your imagination beyond commuters and think about how to make cross-border business more effective and comfortable for employees and employers working in these two cities.

Solution domains we appreciate

  • Commuter journey and customer experience: Make commuter experience as pleasant as you can, find points for friction and fix them in new innovative ways.
  • Make the commute (more) green: eg. infrastructure environmental monitoring.
  • Logistics and efficiency: Make your journey as less time and effort consuming as possible.
  • Build community: Think of ways to create and foster cross-border startup / business community.
  • Open new business in Estonia/Finland - how to lower barrier of start business in a new market.
  • Solutions could be eg. Apps for ferries, Recruitment apps, matchmaking apps, environmental awareness programs, community communication solutions, cross-border business acceleration, add-ons to map solutions, improvements to existing travel sites/solutions/apps, especially services that are missing from current commuter experience.

Challenge

Modern buildings provide huge amounts of data which could be utilized way more than they are today. This data can be static such as 2D floor plans or 3D building information models or dynamic such as IoT data from different sensors (temperature, humidity, CO2, energy consumption etc.). AI and machine learning can be used for proactive maintenance, optimizing performance or better user experience of these buildings. You will have access to four types of buildings which all provide various opportunities to make them more intelligent: school, shopping mall, office building, and university building. Think outside of the box and show us what you can do with this data to take these buildings to the next level!

Solution domains we appreciate

  • Proactive building maintenance and uninterrupted operations based on sensor data and expected life cycle of building parts and use of spaces.
  • Automated optimization of building performance based on the actual use of spaces.
  • Better user experience of buildings based on interaction and wayfinding.

Description of use cases from which real life data is collected:

  • School
  • Shopping Mall
  • Office Building
  • University
